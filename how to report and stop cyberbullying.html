How to Report and Stop Cyberbullying

Information: Steps to take if you're being cyberbullied (blocking, reporting, documenting the incident)

Don’t Respond

- Avoid engaging with the bully. Responding can sometimes escalate the situation.

Block the Bully

- Use the block feature on social media, messaging apps, or gaming platforms to prevent the bully from contacting you further.

Document Everything

- Take screenshots of messages, posts, or any evidence of cyberbullying. Save the dates, times, and details of incidents. This documentation can be crucial for reporting.

Report the Incident

- Report the bullying to the platform where it occurred. Most social media sites and apps have policies against bullying and will take action.

- If the bullying involves threats or harassment, consider reporting it to local authorities or school officials.

Talk to Someone

- Share your experience with a trusted friend, family member, or teacher. It’s important to have support and not face the situation alone.

Reach Out for Help

- Consider contacting a counselor or mental health professional for support. They can provide coping strategies and help you deal with the emotional impact.
