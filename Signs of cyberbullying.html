Signs of Cyberbullying

Change in Behavior

- Sudden changes in mood, such as increased irritability or sadness.

- Withdrawal from friends and activities they once enjoyed.

Avoiding School or Social Events

- Reluctance to go to school or participate in social gatherings.

- Frequent requests to stay home or unexplained absences.

Decline in Academic Performance

- Drop in grades or loss of interest in schoolwork.

- Difficulty concentrating or completing assignments.

Changes in Online Activity

- Increased secrecy about online activities, such as hiding screens or deleting messages.

- Spending excessive time online or being withdrawn from social interactions.

Physical Symptoms

- Complaints of headaches, stomachaches, or other unexplained physical issues.

- Changes in sleep patterns, including insomnia or excessive sleeping.

Emotional Distress

- Expressions of hopelessness, worthlessness, or anxiety.

- Crying spells or unexplained emotional outbursts.

Reluctance to Use Devices

- Avoiding technology or showing fear when using phones or computers.

- Changes in device usage, like sudden disinterest or excessive use.

Decline in Self-Esteem

- Negative comments about themselves or feelings of inadequacy.

- Increased self-criticism or feelings of not fitting in.

Unexplained Changes in Friendships

- Losing friends or feeling isolated from peer groups.

- Signs of conflict with friends or changes in social circles.

Talking About Self-Harm or Suicide

- Mentioning thoughts of self-harm or suicide.

- Expressing feelings of being trapped or having no way out.
